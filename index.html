Optimizing Mangosteen Storage via Derivatives

Predicting Peak Carbon Dioxide ($CO_2$) Respiration Rates in *Garcinia mangostana* Post-Harvest.

The Bioscience Problem

In Agro-Product Technology, managing the post-harvest shelf life of Malaysian fruits like the mangosteen is vital. As a climacteric fruit, its respiration rate spiked drastically during ripening.

By mapping $CO_2$ production over days at a standard tropical ambient temperature ($25^\circ\text{C}$ to $30^\circ\text{C}$), we can use calculus to pinpoint exactly when the ripening acceleration peaks ($f''(x) = 0$) and when respiration maxes out ($f'(x) = 0$). This allows processors to dynamically adjust modified atmosphere packaging (MAP) systems.

The Mathematical Model

A cubic polynomial models the climacteric respiratory curve over 7 days:

Respiration Function $f(x)$:
$f(x) = -0.8x^3 + 7.2x^2 - 12x + 35$

$f(x)$ = $CO_2$ production rate in $\text{mg } CO_2 \cdot \text{kg}^{-1}\cdot\text{h}^{-1}$ at day $x$.

Rate of Change $f'(x)$ (Derivative):
$f'(x) = -2.4x^2 + 14.4x - 12$

$f'(x)$ = Acceleration/Deceleration of gas emission ($\text{mg } CO_2 \cdot \text{kg}^{-1}\cdot\text{h}^{-1}/\text{day}$).
